=== SECTION: Dashboard Telltale / Warning Lights Overview (Manual Page 66 — image only) ===

The Mahindra BE6 Driver Information Display (instrument cluster) shows telltale warning lights.
Telltale lights are grouped into slots and cycle based on their active status.

IMPORTANT: The Mahindra BE6 is a fully electric vehicle. It has NO combustion engine.
There is no traditional "check engine" light. What drivers may call an "engine light" is
actually a powertrain/motor warning indicator or another system fault telltale.

General rules for ALL dashboard telltale warning lights on the BE6:
- At ignition (vehicle start): All telltale lights briefly illuminate for a self-check. They should go out within a few seconds. This is normal.
- If any warning light stays ON or starts BLINKING while driving: This indicates a system fault. The vehicle needs inspection.
- Red warning light: Serious or critical fault. Stop driving as soon as it is safe. Contact Mahindra Roadside Assistance at 1800 266 7070.
- Amber/Yellow warning light: Non-critical fault. Get the vehicle inspected soon. Book a service at a Mahindra Authorised Dealer.
- Green/Blue indicator light: Informational only (e.g., turn signal active, high beam on). Not a fault.

If a driver reports a blinking or illuminated warning light on the dashboard, do NOT guess what it means. Instead:
1. Ask which color the light is (red, amber/yellow, green, blue, white)
2. Ask if it is on the dashboard inside the car, on the charge port at the rear, or on the wall charger unit
3. Advise based on color: red = stop driving soon, amber = get it checked, green/blue = informational

=== SECTION: ABS Warning Light (Manual Page 72) ===

The ABS (Anti-lock Brake System) warning lamp lights up when you switch ON the ignition and should go out after a few seconds as part of the normal self-check.
If the ABS warning lamp does NOT go out after ignition, or if it comes ON while driving, it means there is a fault in the ABS system.
Action required: Visit a Mahindra Authorised Dealer for inspection immediately. Do not ignore an ABS warning light.

=== SECTION: Complete Blinking Light Reference — All Types on the BE6 ===

There are FOUR different locations where lights can blink on the Mahindra BE6. When a driver says "lights are blinking" you MUST ask which one they mean:

--- 1. EXTERIOR / HAZARD LAMPS (outside the car, turn signal lamps) ---
- Hazard lamps flash ONCE: All doors locked and armed successfully via key fob. Normal.
- Hazard lamps flash TWICE: Vehicle unlocked — no theft detected while car was locked. Normal.
- Hazard lamps flash FOUR TIMES + alarm horn: Theft attempt detected while car was locked. Check vehicle.
- Hazard lamps flash FIVE TIMES + alarm horn: A door or bonnet was open when you tried to lock. Close all doors and try again.
- All turn signal lamps flashing continuously: Hazard warning lamp is switched ON (emergency mode). The switch is on the roof console.
- Hazard lamps flash automatically during panic braking: Normal safety feature to warn following traffic. Lasts about 10 seconds.
- Hazard lamps turn on automatically when bonnet is open and vehicle is stationary: Normal safety feature.
- Hazard lamps turn on for 30 minutes after airbag deployment in an accident: Can be turned off manually via the hazard switch.

--- 2. CHARGE PORT LED (small LED on the rear-left charge flap of the car) ---
- White (steady ON): Battery status indication. Normal.
- Red (BLINKING): CHARGE ERROR — something is wrong with the charging connection or system. Disconnect and reconnect the charger. If it persists, contact service.
- Amber (BLINKING): GUN STUCK — the charging plug is locked and will not release. Do not force it. Contact service.
- Green (animation/pulsing): Charging in progress. Normal.

--- 3. EVSE WALL CHARGER LED (light on the portable 3kW charger unit, NOT on the car) ---
- White (steady ON): Charger is ready / battery status.
- White (BLINKING): System Verification — the charger is performing its startup check. Wait for it to complete.
- Blue (BLINKING): Scheduled charging — the car is set to charge at a later time.
- Red (steady ON): Low battery, State of Charge below 10%.
- Red (BLINKING): Charge Error on the charger side.
- Amber (BLINKING): Gun Stuck on the charger side.

--- 4. DASHBOARD TELLTALE LIGHTS (inside the car, instrument cluster) ---
- Any warning light ON at ignition that goes out in a few seconds: Normal self-check.
- Any warning light that STAYS ON or BLINKS while driving: System fault detected. Requires inspection.
- Red telltale while driving: CRITICAL — stop driving as soon as safe, call Mahindra RSA at 1800 266 7070.
- Amber/Yellow telltale while driving: NON-CRITICAL — schedule service soon at a Mahindra Authorised Dealer.
- ABS warning light stays on or comes on while driving: Fault in the ABS system. Visit dealer immediately.

=== SECTION: What to do when you see a warning light ===

When any warning light appears on the dashboard while driving the BE6:
1. Do NOT panic. Reduce speed gradually.
2. Avoid hard acceleration or aggressive driving.
3. If the light is RED: Pull over safely as soon as possible. Do not continue driving.
4. If the light is AMBER/YELLOW: You can continue driving carefully to your destination or a service center.
5. Contact Mahindra Roadside Assistance: 1800 266 7070 (toll-free, 24/7).
6. Visit a Mahindra Authorised Dealer for diagnosis and repair.
7. Do NOT attempt to reset warning lights yourself — they indicate real faults that need professional inspection.

=== SECTION: CID vs DID — Centre Information Display vs Driver Information Display ===

The Mahindra BE6 has TWO screens:

1. CID (Centre Information Display) — the large touchscreen in the center of the dashboard.
   This is the INFOTAINMENT screen. It shows maps, music, settings, phone, apps.
   WARNING LIGHTS DO NOT APPEAR ON THE CID. It is NOT a warning indicator.

2. DID (Driver Information Display) — the instrument cluster screen directly behind the steering wheel.
   This is where ALL telltale/warning lights appear (red, amber, green, blue indicators).
   When a driver says "warning light on my dashboard," they mean the DID.

Important distinction for diagnostics:
- If a driver says "something is showing on my screen" → ask whether they mean the centre touchscreen (infotainment/CID) or the instrument cluster behind the steering wheel (DID).
- Warning/fault telltales ONLY appear on the DID (instrument cluster).
- The CID (touchscreen) may show notifications or alerts from the vehicle's software, but these are NOT the same as telltale warning lights.

=== SECTION: Mahindra BE6 Range, Battery, and Charging Specifications ===

The Mahindra BE6 is available in TWO battery variants:

--- 79 kWh Battery Pack (Long Range) ---
- ARAI certified range: 682 km (ideal/test conditions)
- Real-world range (mixed city + highway): 420–450 km
- Real-world range (pure highway at 80–100 km/h): 380–420 km
- Real-world range (pure city driving): 450–500 km
- Usable battery capacity: ~77.5 kWh

--- 59 kWh Battery Pack (Standard Range) ---
- ARAI certified range: 535 km (ideal/test conditions)
- Real-world range (mixed city + highway): 350–380 km
- Real-world range (pure highway at 80–100 km/h): 300–340 km
- Real-world range (pure city driving): 370–410 km
- Usable battery capacity: ~57 kWh

--- Charging Times ---
DC Fast Charging (up to 175 kW):
- 20% to 80%: approximately 20 minutes
- Each DC fast charge session (20% → 80%) adds approximately 280–320 km of real-world range (79 kWh) or 200–250 km (59 kWh)

AC Home Charging (7.2 kW wallbox):
- 0% to 100%: approximately 10–11 hours (79 kWh) or 8–9 hours (59 kWh)

Portable EVSE (3.3 kW, included with car):
- 0% to 100%: approximately 22–24 hours (79 kWh) or 17–18 hours (59 kWh)

--- Trip Planning Guide ---
When a driver asks "how many charges do I need for X km?", use this formula:
1. Ask which variant they have (79 kWh or 59 kWh) if not known.
2. Assume real-world mixed range: 420 km (79 kWh) or 350 km (59 kWh).
3. If trip distance ≤ range: 0 charging stops needed. Start with a full battery.
4. If trip distance > range: divide remaining km by ~300 km per DC fast charge stop.
5. Each DC fast charge stop takes about 20 minutes (20% to 80%).

Examples:
- 400 km trip, 79 kWh variant: 0 charges needed (420 km range covers it). Start full.
- 400 km trip, 59 kWh variant: 1 DC fast charge stop needed (~20 min at the halfway point).
- 600 km trip, 79 kWh variant: 1 DC fast charge stop needed.
- 600 km trip, 59 kWh variant: 1–2 DC fast charge stops needed.
- 1000 km trip, 79 kWh variant: 2–3 DC fast charge stops needed (total ~60 min charging).

IMPORTANT: Always recommend starting with a FULL battery for long trips. Real-world range varies with speed, AC usage, terrain, and driving style. Highway driving at 100+ km/h reduces range by 10–15%.

=== SECTION: Mahindra BE6 is an Electric Vehicle — No Engine ===

The Mahindra BE6 is a Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV). It does NOT have a petrol or diesel engine.
It is powered by an electric motor and a lithium-ion battery pack.
Therefore, there is no "check engine light" in the traditional sense.
When a driver refers to an "engine light," they likely mean one of:
- A powertrain/motor fault indicator on the dashboard
- A battery management system warning
- A general system fault telltale
The correct response is to ask the driver what color the light is and where it appears (dashboard, charge port, or charger unit).
